About Me

You are going to fall in love with coding

Hello, my name is Erol Ramacik. I was born in Pristina Kosovo in 1991, and there I studied until high school. During that time I participated in multiple math contests and have achieved third as well as first twice. After finishing my high school diploma I sought out further education and studied Computer Engineering at the Ege University in Izmir Turkey. While in university I participated in a contest with a few of my classmates and scored second place with a project called the Fatigue Detection System (See: Projects Section).

Live in coding like you never have before

Throughout my career I have worked as a translator, guide, supervisor, as well as a vice president of marketing. Throughout my life I realised that it isn’t about the fun and games, instead life is about the lessons learned and the contributions made to the world. Alongside that mentality, I have tried to learn many different things, not only in the fields that I am passionate about, but also in the fields that I have been biased against. I am constantly looking to better myself and to learn more. I have come to the conclusion that through the process of learning even the hardest things, I will get soon used to it and will thank myself in the future. It is not important to be all knowing and interested in everything, but instead to try a hand in everything. Through this amazing experience called life it has been summarized with one great quote. As the mathematician and physicist Albert Einstein said “A person who never made a mistake, never tried anything new” and so here I am.

Fatigue Detection System

This is the System (Program) that detects Fatigue status of drivers and gives a warning before sleep. We aimed to prevent vital damage or accidents by alerting drivers or taking over the driver's control. The performance of the camera connected to the system is at a lower cost compared to the devices and systems in the literature. It was tested on people working in the development of the system in a laboratory environment at different light levels.
